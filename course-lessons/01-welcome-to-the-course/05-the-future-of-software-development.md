# AI Is The Future

So, just a few more things I want to touch on before we get started. I know there's some resistance to AI coding tools and rightly so. However, AI and these tools and the whole workflow is not going anywhere. It's not a fad like some of these other things that fizzled out.

Just to talk about some of these things for a minute. I'm sure that you remember the Web3, NFT, Crypto boom? I'm not saying crypto is useless. It has its place. But remember when we thought everything was gonna move to a decentralized platform? Some of the technology was and is pretty cool, but you never hear anyone talk about Web3 and NFTs in the mainstream anymore. We did a lot of speculation but underestimated the actual utility.

Another example is the Metaverse. People were gonna start having their work meetings in VR, digital offices. That crashed around 2023. The was hype wasn't meeting reality. Google Glass - remember that? Privacy backlash, just wasn't practical. Dead within a few years. Blockchain everything - this lines up with the first one and everything being decentralized.

So why am I bringing this up? Because I want you to understand the difference between hype cycles and actual technological shifts. And AI? AI is fundamentally different.

Every major tech company is investing billions into it. Microsoft with GitHub Copilot, Google with Gemini, Anthropic with Claude. Of course, OpenAI and GPT - these aren't experiments, they're core products. They're solving real problems right now.

I've seen a lot of trends come and go. I've watched people go all-in on technologies that ended up being dead ends. But AI tools are different because they're already integrated into the workflow of millions of developers. They're not some future promise.

In my opinion, the developers who resist learning these tools aren't protecting their careers, they're limiting them. It's like refusing to learn Git because you're really good with SVN, or refusing to use a code editor because you're fast with Notepad. AI doesn't replace your skills, it leverages them.

A lot of people fear AI taking jobs, and yes it will take some of them. It already has, but ultimately, I think it will change the landscape. Instead of developers being expected to do x today, their going to be expected to do x, y and z.

Companies are already expecting developers to be productive with AI tools. Job descriptions are starting to mention it. Teams are adopting these tools as standard practice. In a few years, not knowing how to work effectively with AI as a developer will be like not knowing how to use version control.

The developers who thrive in the next five to ten years won't be the ones who can code the fastest or memorize the most syntax. It'll be the ones who can solve problems, understand systems, and effectively leverage AI tools to build better software faster.

So yeah, be skeptical. Be critical of the hype. But don't ignore it. Learn to use these tools properly, and you'll have a massive advantage over developers who refuse to adapt.
